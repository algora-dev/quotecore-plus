# Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

**Effective date:** 17 July 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service ("Terms") between T3 Play Limited ("we", "us", "Processor") and you ("you", "Customer") and applies where we process personal data on your behalf as a data processor.

Capitalised terms not defined here have the meaning given in the Terms.

---

## 1. Scope and roles

### 1.1 Roles

You are the data controller (or processor acting on instructions of a controller). We are the data processor acting on your instructions.

### 1.2 Subject matter

This DPA covers the processing of personal data contained in Customer Data that you submit to the Service.

### 1.3 Compliance with laws

Each party complies with its obligations under applicable data protection laws, including the GDPR, UK GDPR, the Privacy Act 2020 (New Zealand), the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) (Australia), and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) where applicable.

---

## 2. Processing details

### 2.1 Purpose

We process personal data only for the purpose of providing, securing and supporting the Service as described in the Terms.

### 2.2 Data types

The personal data we process on your behalf may include:

- customer names, email addresses, postal addresses and phone numbers entered into quotes, orders and invoices;
- business contact details of your customers and suppliers;
- file attachments (plans, documents) that may contain personal data; and
- any other personal data you choose to submit to the Service.

### 2.3 Data subjects

Data subjects may include your customers, prospects, suppliers, employees and other individuals whose personal data you submit to the Service.

### 2.4 Processing location

Processing occurs in Australia (Supabase, Sydney), the United States (Vercel, Resend, OpenAI, Stripe) and New Zealand (T3 Play Limited operations).

---

## 3. Your obligations

You warrant that:

- you have a lawful basis for processing the personal data you submit to the Service;
- you have obtained all necessary consents and provided all required notices to data subjects;
- your instructions to us regarding processing are lawful; and
- you will not submit special-category personal data (as defined by GDPR Article 9) to the Service unless you have a lawful basis to do so and have informed the data subjects.

---

## 4. Our obligations

### 4.1 Instructions

We will process personal data only on your documented instructions, including with regard to transfers of personal data to a third country, unless required by law. We will inform you if we believe an instruction infringes applicable data protection law.

### 4.2 Confidentiality

We ensure that personnel authorised to process personal data are subject to confidentiality obligations.

### 4.3 Security measures

We implement the technical and organisational security measures described in the Terms (section 13) and in our Privacy Policy, including:

- encryption in transit and at rest;
- access controls and authentication;
- row-level security on the database;
- rate-limiting on security-sensitive operations;
- logging and monitoring; and
- regular backups.

### 4.4 Subprocessors

We use the subprocessors listed in the Terms (section 11.2). We will give you at least 30 days' notice of material changes to subprocessors. You may object to a new subprocessor on reasonable data-protection grounds by notifying us in writing within 30 days of receiving notice.

### 4.5 Data subject requests

We will assist you in responding to data subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection) by providing functionality within the Service for you to access, export and delete Customer Data. Where a data subject contacts us directly, we will redirect them to you.

### 4.6 Breach notification

We will notify you without undue delay (and in any case within 72 hours) of becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting your Customer Data. We will provide:

- a description of the breach;
- the likely consequences;
- the measures taken or proposed to address it; and
- contact details for obtaining further information.

We will cooperate with you in meeting any breach-notification obligations you have under applicable law.

### 4.7 Data protection impact assessments

We will provide reasonable assistance to you in conducting data protection impact assessments where required by GDPR Article 35.

---

## 5. International data transfers

### 5.1 Transfers outside the EEA/UK/Switzerland

Where personal data is transferred from the EEA, UK or Switzerland to a country that has not been recognised as providing an adequate level of protection, we rely on:

- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as adopted by the European Commission (Decision 2021/914) and the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or UK Addendum to the EU SCCs, as applicable; or
- another lawful transfer mechanism under applicable data protection law.

### 5.2 Subprocessor transfers

We ensure that subprocessors processing personal data outside the EEA/UK/Switzerland are bound by appropriate safeguards, including SCCs or equivalent mechanisms.

---

## 6. Deletion and return

### 6.1 On termination

Upon termination or expiry of your subscription, and after the data retention period in the Terms (section 12), we will delete your Customer Data from active systems in accordance with our data retention policy.

### 6.2 Backups

Personal data may remain in encrypted backups for up to 90 days after deletion from active systems, after which it is overwritten through the ordinary backup retention cycle.

### 6.3 Legal retention

We may retain personal data where required by law, subject to the protections of this DPA.

---

## 7. Audit rights

You may audit our compliance with this DPA, subject to:

- giving at least 30 days' written notice;
- conducting the audit during normal business hours;
- not unreasonably disrupting our operations;
- maintaining confidentiality of any information obtained; and
- using a qualified independent auditor who is not a competitor.

We will provide reasonable cooperation and access to relevant documentation. We will make available information demonstrating our compliance, including audit reports, certifications and summaries of security assessments, where available.

---

## 8. Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations in the Terms (section 24).

---

## 9. Term and termination

This DPA takes effect on the effective date and remains in force for as long as we process personal data on your behalf. Upon termination, the deletion and retention provisions survive.

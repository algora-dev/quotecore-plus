# Glossary

Short definitions of the QuoteCore+ terms you will see in the app.

## QuoteCore+ terms

**Actual measurement.** A real-world number. The opposite of a [plan measurement](/docs/concepts/plan-vs-actual). Pitch is skipped; waste still applies.

**Component.** A re-usable line you put in quotes. Has a name, measurement type, material price, labor price, waste, and optional pitch/angle settings. See [Components overview](/docs/components/overview).

**Customer quote.** The version of the quote your customer sees. Built from the master quote summary in the [customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor).

**Digital takeoff.** Measuring directly off a digital plan or aerial image. See [Digital takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/digital-takeoff).

**Extra.** A line you want priced and recorded but kept separate from the main components. Skip hire, scaffold, delivery, accommodation. See [Components vs Extras](/docs/concepts/components-vs-extras).

**Labor sheet.** The version of the quote your fixers or installers see. Same lines, with labor amounts. See [Labor sheet editor](/docs/labor-and-installers/labor-sheet-editor).

**Main component.** A regular component, as opposed to an Extra. Most things you quote are Main.

**Manual quote.** A quote built line-by-line for users who already have their takeoff values. See [Manual Quote](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-quote).

**Pitch multiplier.** A factor applied to a plan measurement to convert it to the actual material length or area, accounting for slope or angle. Set on the component. See [Waste and pitch](/docs/concepts/waste-and-pitch).

**Plan measurement.** A flat number off a plan view or aerial image, before the pitch multiplier is applied. See [Plan vs Actual](/docs/concepts/plan-vs-actual).

**Quote builder.** The four-phase flow that builds a quote: Areas, Components, Extras, Review. See [Quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder).

**Quote summary.** The master view of a quote. Everything in it, with margins applied. See [Quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).

**Requote.** A request from a customer for a fresh quote with changes. Triggered from the customer quote URL. See [Sending and acceptance](/docs/customer-facing/sending-and-acceptance).

**Takeoff.** The act of measuring a job. Either digital (off a plan) or manual (on site).

**Withdrawn quote.** A quote you have pulled back. The customer URL still works but tells them the quote is no longer valid. See [Withdrawing a quote](/docs/customer-facing/withdrawing-a-quote).

## Billing terms

**Cap.** A numeric limit on something your plan allows - quotes per month, components total, storage bytes, etc. Hitting a cap blocks new additions but never deletes existing data. See [Tier limits](/docs/account/tier-limits).

**Dunning.** The process Stripe (and we) follow when a renewal payment fails: automatic card retries, status escalation through `past_due` - `grace` - `suspended`, with chances to recover by updating the card. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Effective plan.** What your account can actually use today. Usually the same as your purchased plan, but collapses to **Starter** during grace periods or after the trial ends.

**Entitlement.** The bundle of features and caps your plan unlocks. Every gated action (creating a quote, drawing a flashing) checks the entitlement before allowing it.

**Grace period.** A 10-day window after a payment failure where you keep read access but can't create new quotes. Buys you time to update the card before suspension. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Launch pricing.** The current discounted price we're offering during the early phase. Locked in for the lifetime of the subscription - renewals stay at the launch price.

**MSRP.** The original list price shown as a strikethrough next to the launch price. What we'll charge once launch pricing ends.

**Purchased plan.** The plan you actually paid for. Stays constant during payment failures - only changes when you explicitly switch plans.

**Tier.** Synonym for plan. Trial, Starter, Growth, Professional, Pro Plus, Premium.

**Trial.** A 14-day free taste of every feature, with tiny caps. One per account, ever. See [Free trial](/docs/account/trial).

**Winding down.** A paid subscription that's been cancelled but is still serving out the paid period. Status is technically still `active`, but it will collapse to Starter at the period end.

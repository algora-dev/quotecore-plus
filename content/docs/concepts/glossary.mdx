# Glossary

Short definitions of the QuoteCore+ and roofing terms you will see in the app.

## QuoteCore+ terms

**Actual measurement.** A real-world number. The opposite of a [plan measurement](/docs/concepts/plan-vs-actual). Pitch is skipped; waste still applies.

**Component.** A re-usable line you put in quotes. Has a name, measurement type, material price, labor price, waste, and optional pitch settings. See [Components overview](/docs/components/overview).

**Customer quote.** The version of the quote your customer sees. Built from the master quote summary in the [customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor).

**Digital takeoff.** Measuring directly off a digital plan or aerial image. See [Digital takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/digital-takeoff).

**Extra.** A line you want priced and recorded but kept separate from the main components. Skip hire, scaffold, delivery, accommodation. See [Components vs Extras](/docs/concepts/components-vs-extras).

**Labor sheet.** The version of the quote your fixers or installers see. Same lines, with labor amounts. See [Labor sheet editor](/docs/labor-and-installers/labor-sheet-editor).

**Main component.** A regular component, as opposed to an Extra. Most things you quote are Main.

**Manual quote.** A quote built line-by-line for trades that do not need takeoff or pitch maths. See [Manual quote](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-quote).

**Manual takeoff.** A quote built using saved components and your own measurements, with no digital plan involved. See [Manual takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-takeoff).

**Plan measurement.** A flat number off a roof plan or aerial image, before pitch is applied. See [Plan vs Actual](/docs/concepts/plan-vs-actual).

**Quote builder.** The four-phase flow that builds a quote: Areas, Components, Extras, Review. See [Quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder).

**Quote summary.** The master view of a quote. Everything in it, with margins applied. See [Quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).

**Requote.** A request from a customer for a fresh quote with changes. Triggered from the customer quote URL. See [Sending and acceptance](/docs/customer-facing/sending-and-acceptance).

**Takeoff.** The act of measuring a job. Either digital (off a plan) or manual (on site).

**Withdrawn quote.** A quote you have pulled back. The customer URL still works but tells them the quote is no longer valid. See [Withdrawing a quote](/docs/customer-facing/withdrawing-a-quote).

## Roofing terms

**Barge.** The flashing along the gable end of a roof - the angled edge.

**Downpipe.** The vertical pipe that takes water from the gutter to the ground.

**Fascia.** The horizontal board (often metal) along the eaves where the gutter mounts.

**Fixings.** Screws, nails, clips, brackets - anything that holds materials in place.

**Gutter / Spouting.** The horizontal channel along the eaves that catches roof water.

**Hip.** The external angle where two roof slopes meet, running from ridge to eave.

**Longrun.** Long-length metal roofing iron, run from ridge to eave in a single sheet.

**Rafter pitch.** The pitch type for the main roof slope. Covers tiles, longrun, underlay.

**Ridge.** The horizontal line at the top of the roof where two slopes meet. Also the flashing that covers it.

**Underlay.** The membrane laid under the roofing material for water protection.

**Valley.** The internal angle where two roof slopes meet, running from ridge to gutter.

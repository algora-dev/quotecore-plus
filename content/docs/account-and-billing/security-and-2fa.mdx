# Security and 2FA

Your account is the door to every quote, customer, and price you've stored. The security page is where you keep that door locked.

## When to use it

- First-time setup - turn on two-factor.
- Updating your password.
- Adding or refreshing recovery codes.
- Setting recovery questions.

## What's on the page

### Password

Change your password at any time. Pick something long and unique. We recommend a password manager.

### Two-factor authentication (2FA)

Two-factor adds a one-time code to your login - even someone with your password can't log in without your phone or authenticator app.

To turn it on:

1. Click **Enable 2FA**.
2. Scan the QR code with an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, anything that does TOTP).
3. Enter the six-digit code to confirm.
4. Save your recovery codes somewhere safe.

If you ever lose your phone, the recovery codes get you back in.

### Recovery codes

A set of one-time-use codes you can use if you lose access to your authenticator. Save them somewhere outside the app - password manager, printed, locked drawer.

You can regenerate a new set any time. Doing so invalidates the old set.

### Recovery questions

Optional. A second layer for account recovery if everything else fails - you set a few questions and answers only you'd know.

## Email change limit

Your account email can only be changed once every seven days. This is a security limit, not a bug. If you need to change it twice in a week, contact support.

## Tips

- Always turn on 2FA. It's one of the easiest ways to lock your account down.
- Keep recovery codes printed somewhere physical. If your password manager fails, you don't want digital recovery to also fail.
- Don't reuse the password from another service.

<Callout type="warning">
If you lose your 2FA device, lose your recovery codes, and can't answer your recovery questions, getting back in becomes manual and slow. Support can help, but it's not instant. Set everything up now while you don't need it.
</Callout>

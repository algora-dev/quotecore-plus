# Company settings

The company page is where you set the things customers see on quotes - your name, logo, contact details - and the defaults the app uses when you build a new quote.

## When to use it

- First-time setup. See [Set up your company](/docs/getting-started/set-up-your-company) for the walkthrough.
- Updating logo, address, or contact details.
- Changing your default currency or measurement system.
- Adding or editing tax rates.
- Adjusting default profit margins.

## What's on the page

### Company info

- **Company name** - what appears on customer quotes.
- **Logo** - top-right of the customer quote. Transparent PNG works best.
- **Address** - shown on the customer quote header.
- **Phone** - shown on the customer quote header.
- **Company email** - shown on the customer quote (overrides your account email).

### Defaults for new quotes

- **Default currency** - three-letter code. Existing quotes keep their original currency.
- **Default measurement system** - metric, imperial feet, or imperial roofing squares. Existing quotes lock to the system they were created in.
- **Default material margin** - percentage applied to materials on a new quote.
- **Default labor margin** - percentage applied to labor on a new quote.

### Taxes

A list of tax rows. Each row has:

- **Name** - shown on the customer quote (e.g. "GST", "VAT").
- **Rate %** - the percentage.
- **Show on customer quote** - toggle.
- **Show on labor sheet** - toggle.

New quotes copy the tax list when they're created, so editing the list later doesn't change quotes you've already made.

## Editing taxes mid-quote

If you need to update a tax on an existing quote, edit it inside the quote's tax panel - not the company tax list. Company tax changes only apply to new quotes.

## Tips

- Set realistic default margins. They're meant to be the typical case, not the maximum or minimum.
- For multi-tax regions (federal + provincial), add each tax as its own row. The customer quote shows them as separate lines.
- If you change the company name, existing quotes keep the old name on them. Re-generate the customer quote to pick up the new name.

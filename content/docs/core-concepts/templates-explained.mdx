# Templates explained

Templates are saved presets you load when you start a quote so you don't rebuild the same thing every time. There are four kinds. They do different jobs.

## Quote templates

A starter set of components for a type of job. If you quote a lot of corrugate longrun roofs, your template might already include the longrun, ridges, valleys, hips, fixings, underlay, and a delivery fee. You load it and start measuring instead of starting from a blank quote.

Quote templates work with manual takeoff. They don't apply to digital takeoff because in digital takeoff you decide what to measure on the plan as you go.

See [Quote templates](/docs/templates/quote-templates).

## Customer quote templates

The layout the customer sees - logo, header text, footer disclaimers, contact details. You can have one for residential, one for commercial, one for re-roofs, whatever makes sense.

You pick a customer quote template from the dropdown when you open the customer quote editor.

See [Customer quote templates](/docs/templates/customer-quote-templates).

## Email templates

The email that delivers the quote. Subject line, body text, and placeholders that fill in for each customer (their name, your company name, the quote number, the link).

Useful when you send a lot of quotes a week. Set one as your default and you don't think about it.

See [Email templates](/docs/templates/email-templates).

## Labor sheet templates

The header and footer of the labor sheet your fixers see. Same idea as customer quote templates but for the install version of the document. Site address, scope, and your installer's contact - whatever you want them to see at a glance.

See [Labor sheet templates](/docs/templates/labor-sheet-templates).

## When you'd use which

- New job with the same components as your usual work: **Quote template**.
- Different customer audience (residential vs commercial): **Customer quote template**.
- Stop typing the same email: **Email template**.
- Different install info for different fixers or sites: **Labor sheet template**.

## Tips

- You can edit any line in a quote after loading a template. Templates are starting points, not contracts.
- Mark one of each as default. New quotes pick those up automatically.

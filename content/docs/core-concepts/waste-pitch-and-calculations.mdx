# Waste, pitch, and calculations

A roof plan is flat. The roof isn't. That's why every quote does two adjustments: **pitch** to account for the slope, **waste** to account for cuts, breaks, and overlap.

This page explains both and how they work together.

## Pitch

Pitch turns a flat plan area into the area of actual material you need.

There are three pitch types in QuoteCore+:

- **Rafter pitch** - for the main roof slope. Use it on tiles, longrun, underlay, anything that covers the slope.
- **Hip pitch** - for hip rafters running diagonally across the roof.
- **Valley pitch** - for valleys.

You set the pitch on the **area** in the quote builder, and you tell each component which pitch type to use when you create the component. The maths happens automatically.

<Callout type="note">
Hip and valley pitch currently support 45-degree corners on plan. If your roof has tapered hips or valleys, add length manually or do the maths yourself for those runs.
</Callout>

### How rafter pitch works

The pitch factor is `1 / cos(angle)`. A 30-degree pitch has a factor of about 1.155, so a 100 m2 plan becomes about 115.5 m2 of real material.

You don't need to remember the formula. Set the pitch on the area, set the component to use rafter pitch, and the app handles it.

## Waste

Waste is the buffer you add for cuts, broken sheets, and overlap. There are two types:

- **Percentage** - 5% means add 5% to whatever the calculated quantity is. Good for area-based materials like roof iron.
- **Fixed** - add a fixed amount per item. Good for lineal materials. For example: 100 mm extra per length of ridge.

You set the default waste type and amount when you create the component. You can override it per quote in the [Quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder).

## How they combine

The order is: **plan number > pitch > waste > final quantity**.

| Plan | Pitch factor | Waste | Final |
|---|---|---|---|
| 100 m2 | 1.155 (30 degrees) | 5% | 121.3 m2 |
| 100 m2 | 1.0 (no pitch) | 5% | 105 m2 |
| 50 lm | 1.0 (no pitch on a barge) | 5% | 52.5 lm |
| 50 lm | 1.0 | Fixed 100 mm/length, 10 lengths | 51 lm |

The customer quote price is `final quantity x rate`. Same maths for material price and labor price.

## Setting waste on actual measurements

If a component is set to **Actual** ([Plan vs Actual](/docs/core-concepts/plan-vs-actual)), pitch is skipped. Waste still applies. So a site-measured 50 m of ridge with 5% waste becomes 52.5 m of ridge to order.

That's usually what you want. If you don't, set waste to 0 on that component.

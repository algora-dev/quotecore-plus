# Glossary

The terms QuoteCore+ uses, in plain English. Where we use a roofing word, we explain it.

## Quote and component terms

**Acceptance token.** The unique code in a customer quote URL that tells the app which quote to show when the customer clicks the link. Generated when you send the quote.

**Actual measurement.** A real-world number you measured on site. The opposite of a [plan measurement](/docs/core-concepts/plan-vs-actual). Pitch is skipped for actual; waste still applies.

**Areas (in the quote builder).** Sections of a roof - main, garage, lean-to, dormer. Each has its own pitch and area numbers. See [Quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder).

**Component.** A re-usable line you put in quotes. Has a name, measurement type, material price, labor price, waste, and pitch settings. See [Components overview](/docs/components/overview).

**Components phase.** The middle phase of the quote builder - you add components, set lengths, set quantities. See [Quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder).

**Customer quote.** The version of the quote your customer sees. Built from the master quote summary in the [Customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor).

**Digital takeoff.** Measuring directly off a digital plan or aerial image. See [Digital takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/digital-takeoff).

**Extras.** A line you want priced and recorded but not necessarily ordered or sent to the supplier. Skip hire, scaffold, accommodation. See [Components vs Extras](/docs/core-concepts/components-vs-extras).

**Extras phase.** The third phase of the quote builder - add Extras after components.

**Labor sheet.** The version of the quote your fixers see. Same lines, no markups, with labor amounts. See [Labor sheet editor](/docs/labor-and-installers/labor-sheet-editor).

**Main component.** A regular component, as opposed to an Extra. Most things you quote are Main. See [Components vs Extras](/docs/core-concepts/components-vs-extras).

**Manual quote.** A quote built line-by-line without takeoff or pitch maths. Suits trades that don't need takeoff. See [Manual quote](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-quote).

**Manual takeoff.** A quote built using saved components and your own measurements - no digital plan involved. See [Manual takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-takeoff).

**Pitch.** The angle of the roof slope. Drives how plan measurements are scaled to actual. See [Waste, pitch, and calculations](/docs/core-concepts/waste-pitch-and-calculations).

**Plan measurement.** A flat number off a roof plan, before pitch is applied. See [Plan vs Actual](/docs/core-concepts/plan-vs-actual).

**Quote builder.** The four-phase flow that builds a quote: Areas, Components, Extras, Review. See [Quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder).

**Quote summary.** The master view of a quote. Everything that's in it, with margins applied. See [Quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).

**Requote.** A request from a customer for a fresh quote with changes. Triggered from the customer quote URL. See [Requote requests](/docs/customer-facing/requote-requests).

**Review (in the quote builder).** The last phase before save - where you set margins and look over the maths.

**Takeoff.** The act of measuring a job. Either digital (off a plan) or manual (on site, on paper, then typed in).

**Waste percent.** A percentage added to a calculated quantity to cover cuts and breaks.

**Waste fixed.** A fixed amount added per item. Useful for lineal materials.

**Waste type.** Whether waste is calculated as a percentage or a fixed amount per item.

**Withdrawn quote.** A quote you've pulled back. The customer URL still works but tells the customer the quote is no longer valid. See [Withdrawing a quote](/docs/customer-facing/withdrawing-a-quote).

## Roofing terms

**Barge.** The flashing along the gable end of a roof - the angled edge.

**Downpipe.** The vertical pipe that takes water from the gutter to the ground.

**Fascia.** The horizontal board (often metal) along the eaves where the gutter mounts.

**Fixings.** Screws, nails, clips, brackets - anything that holds materials in place.

**Gutter / Spouting.** The horizontal channel along the eaves that catches roof water.

**Hip.** The external angle where two roof slopes meet, running from ridge to eave.

**Hip pitch.** The pitch type for hip rafters.

**Longrun.** Long-length metal roofing iron, run from ridge to eave in a single sheet.

**Rafter pitch.** The pitch type for the main roof slope - covers tiles, longrun, underlay.

**Ridge.** The horizontal line at the top of the roof where two slopes meet. Also the flashing that covers it.

**Ridge cap.** The flashing component over the ridge.

**Underlay.** The membrane laid under the roofing material for water protection.

**Valley.** The internal angle where two roof slopes meet, running from ridge to gutter.

**Valley pitch.** The pitch type for valley rafters.

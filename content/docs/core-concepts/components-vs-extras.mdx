# Components vs Extras

Every line on a quote is either a **Main** component or an **Extra**. They behave the same for pricing - the split is mostly about how the quote reads and how you keep your records.

## Main components

These are the materials and labor that make up the job. Roofing iron, tiles, ridges, valleys, underlay, fixings - the things you measure, calculate pitch on, and order from a supplier.

If a customer asks "what am I paying for?", these are the answer. Almost everything you put in a quote should be a Main component.

## Extras

Extras are everything else. Use them for:

- One-off costs - skip hire, scaffold, accommodation, dangerous-site allowance.
- Things you want on your records but hidden from the customer - internal notes, contractor markups.
- Optional add-ons you want to price separately so the customer can see the breakdown.
- Catch-all line items that don't fit the component model.

## How they differ in practice

| | Main | Extra |
|---|---|---|
| Where it shows | Quote summary, customer quote, labor sheet | Quote summary, customer quote (if visible), labor sheet (if visible) |
| Pitch and waste calculations | Yes | Yes |
| Material orders | Yes (if component is enabled for orders) | Usually no - extras are typically not on supplier orders |
| Typical use | Roof tiles, longrun, flashings, fixings | Skip hire, site cleanup, accommodation, custom one-offs |

## When to make something an Extra

- It's not on every job. Skip hire and accommodation only show up on certain jobs.
- It's not measured. A "site visit fee" is a flat amount.
- You want it visually separated on the customer quote.

## When to keep it as a Main component

- You order it from a supplier.
- You measure it.
- It's used on most jobs.

<Callout type="tip">
You can hide any line - Main or Extra - from the customer quote without deleting it. Use the show/hide toggle in the [Customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor). The line still counts in your records and on the labor sheet.
</Callout>

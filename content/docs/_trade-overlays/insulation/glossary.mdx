# Glossary

Short definitions of the QuoteCore+ terms and insulation measurement types you will see in the app.

## Insulation measurement types

**Area.** A flat surface measured in m² - ceilings, floors, lofts viewed plan-on, or wall sections viewed face-on. Used for batts, rolls, blown fill, vapour barriers.

**Irregular Area.** An area that isn't a simple rectangle - L-shaped lofts, rooms with bay extensions, multi-pitch ceiling spaces.

**Wall Length × Height (multi_lineal_lxh).** Several wall runs measured plan-view as lengths, with one shared height applied. Quick way to quote multi-wall internal insulation jobs.

**Wall Height × Length (length_x_height).** A single wall measured length × height. Used for one-off wall sections.

**Multiple Edge Runs (multi_lineal).** Several edge sealing or tape runs added together as one component line. Use when the same product appears in several places.

**Lineal.** A single continuous run measured in metres - perimeter edge bead, expanding foam fillet, sealing tape along a wall plate.

**Count.** Individual items - bags of blown fill, packs of batts, downlight covers, fire collars, access hatches.

**Fixed.** A flat amount that doesn't scale with measurement - delivery, access lift hire, minimum call-out, allowances.

**Hours / Days.** Labor billed by time rather than by quantity installed.

## QuoteCore+ terms

**Actual measurement.** The real-world measurement after waste and (if relevant) pitch have been applied to the plan measurement.

**Component.** A reusable insulation item with its own price, pack size, labor rate, and waste settings.

**Customer quote.** The polished, customer-facing version of the quote. For insulation work the default label is **Insulation Works**.

**Digital takeoff.** Tracing measurements directly on an uploaded floor plan or site drawing.

**Extra.** A non-core component bucket for call-outs, allowances, hidden items, or anything outside the main scope.

**Manual quote.** A quote built by typing measurements straight into the quote builder, without a digital takeoff.

**Pitch multiplier.** A factor that converts plan-view roof or loft area into actual slope area for insulation that follows the rafters.

**Plan measurement.** The raw measurement taken from the plan, before waste or pitch is applied.

**Quote builder.** The screen where you assemble areas, components, prices, and notes into the final quote.

**Takeoff.** The list of measurements that drives the quote - whether digital or manual.

## Billing terms

**Cap.** A numeric limit on something your plan allows - quotes per month, components total, storage bytes, etc. Hitting a cap blocks new additions but never deletes existing data. See [Tier limits](/docs/account/tier-limits).

**Dunning.** The process Stripe (and we) follow when a renewal payment fails: automatic card retries, status escalation through `past_due` - `grace` - `suspended`, with chances to recover by updating the card. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Effective plan.** What your account can actually use today. Usually the same as your purchased plan, but collapses to **Starter** during grace periods or after the trial ends.

**Grace period.** A 10-day window after a payment failure where you keep read access but can't create new quotes. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Trial.** A 14-day free taste of every feature, with tiny caps. One per account, ever. See [Free trial](/docs/account/trial).

# Digital takeoff

Upload any scaled floor plan, site drawing, or services layout and measure directly on screen. Set a calibration reference on a known measurement and you can trace pipe runs, mark fixture positions, and calculate areas without manual scaling.

All you need is a reliable scale or defined measurement on that plan that you know is correct. In digital takeoff, the first step is calibrating the measurement system using that scale.

Tip: use a full wall run, room dimension, or slab edge for calibration - these are usually the most reliable measurements on a floor plan.

## What you can measure

- **Pipe runs** - use the Line tool for a single run, or Multi-Line for multiple runs that are summed together.
- **Curved pipe routes** - use the Curved Line tool for bends, sweeps, or pipes following a curved wall or slab edge.
- **Fixture and fitting positions** - use the Point tool for taps, valves, toilets, basins, hot water units, and any item priced per unit.
- **Areas** - trace a room or zone for components priced by floor area (e.g. waterproofing membrane, drainage mat).

All measurements are saved and auto-calculated to pre-populate the next phase in the quote builder.

## What happens next

When you continue from digital takeoff into the [quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder), you will see each area you created, each component you measured, and its associated items. You can edit and adjust if needed, or continue quickly to the next step.

## What gets saved

- Your initial floor plan or drawing.
- A digital takeoff version of the image showing your lines, measurements, areas, and items overlaid on the plan.
- A clean image without your uploaded image and only your digital takeoff lines / items, so you can clearly see what you measured.

These are useful for job records, compliance documentation, or to clearly define exactly what was quoted if you need to reference it later.

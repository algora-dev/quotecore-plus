# Glossary

Short definitions of the QuoteCore+ terms and construction measurement types you will see in the app.

## Construction measurement types

**Area.** A flat surface measured in m² - cladding, sheeting, roofing, decking, lining, slabs.

**Irregular Area.** An area that isn't a simple rectangle - L-shapes, multi-section roofs or walls, raked walls.

**Lineal.** A single continuous run measured in metres - framing, fascia, gutter, trim, formwork, beams.

**Multiple Lines (multi_lineal).** Several lineal runs of the same material added together as one component line.

**Wall Length × Height (multi_lineal_lxh).** Several wall runs measured plan-view as lengths with a shared height. Quick way to quote whole-wall framing or cladding.

**Wall Height × Length (length_x_height).** A single wall measured length × height. Used for one-off walls.

**Volume.** A measurement in m³ - concrete pours, bulk fill, footings.

**Curved Line (curved_line).** A curved run measured along its curve - radius framing, decorative trim, curved fascia.

**Count.** Individual items - windows, doors, fixtures, hardware items, beams, columns.

**Fixed.** A flat amount that doesn't scale with measurement - subcontract allowances, PC sums, site costs, plant hire.

**Hours / Days.** Labor billed by time rather than by quantity installed.

## QuoteCore+ terms

**Actual measurement.** The real-world measurement after waste (and per-component pitch, if used) has been applied to the plan measurement.

**Component.** A reusable construction item with its own price, pack size, labor rate, and waste settings.

**Customer quote.** The polished, customer-facing version of the quote. For construction work the default label is **Construction Works**.

**Digital takeoff.** Tracing measurements directly on an uploaded plan, elevation, or site drawing.

**Extra.** A non-core component bucket for subcontract allowances, PC sums, hidden items, or anything outside the main scope.

**Manual quote.** A quote built by typing measurements straight into the quote builder, without a digital takeoff.

**Pitch multiplier.** A factor that converts plan-view length or area into actual sloped length or area. Available per-component (not at trade level) for construction - turn it on for roof framing or sheeting measured from a plan view.

**Plan measurement.** The raw measurement taken from the plan, before waste or pitch is applied.

**Quote builder.** The screen where you assemble areas, components, prices, and notes into the final quote.

**Takeoff.** The list of measurements that drives the quote - whether digital or manual.

## Billing terms

**Cap.** A numeric limit on something your plan allows - quotes per month, components total, storage bytes, etc. Hitting a cap blocks new additions but never deletes existing data. See [Tier limits](/docs/account/tier-limits).

**Dunning.** The process Stripe (and we) follow when a renewal payment fails: automatic card retries, status escalation through `past_due` - `grace` - `suspended`, with chances to recover by updating the card. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Effective plan.** What your account can actually use today. Usually the same as your purchased plan, but collapses to **Starter** during grace periods or after the trial ends.

**Grace period.** A 10-day window after a payment failure where you keep read access but can't create new quotes. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Trial.** A 14-day free taste of every feature, with tiny caps. One per account, ever. See [Free trial](/docs/account/trial).

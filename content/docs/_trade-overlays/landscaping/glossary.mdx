# Glossary

## Landscaping measurement types

**Area.** A flat surface measured in m². Lawns, patios, decking, paving.

**Irregular Area.** An area with a non-rectangular shape, traced as a polygon. Shaped lawns, planting beds, organic patios.

**Lineal.** A single run measured in metres. Edging, kerb, a single retaining wall run, an irrigation main.

**Multiple Lines.** Several lineal runs gathered into one component — useful for several edging runs around different beds.

**Wall Length × Height (multi_lineal_lxh).** Walls measured as a list of runs with individual heights — handy for stepped retaining walls.

**Wall Height × Length (length_x_height).** A single wall face measured by its height and length. Used for sleeper walls or block walls.

**Volume.** Measured in m³. Topsoil, mulch, aggregate, sand, gravel, fill.

**Curved Line.** A flowing or sweeping line — bed borders, curved paths, organic edging.

**Count.** A whole-number tally — plants, trees, shrubs, sprinkler heads, posts.

**Fixed.** A flat-fee line — design packages, site visit fees, callout charges.

**Hours / Days.** Time-based pricing for labor crews, machine hire, day-rate teams.

## QuoteCore+ terms

**Actual measurement.** A real-world number entered directly. Slope is skipped; waste still applies.

**Component.** A re-usable line you put in quotes. Has a name, measurement type, material price, labor price, waste, and optional slope settings. See [Components overview](/docs/components/overview).

**Customer quote.** The version of the quote your customer sees, labelled **Landscaping Works**. See [customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor).

**Digital takeoff.** Measuring directly off a digital site plan or drawing. See [Digital takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/digital-takeoff).

**Extra.** A line you want priced and recorded but kept separate from the main components. See [Components vs Extras](/docs/concepts/components-vs-extras).

**Manual quote.** A quote built line-by-line for users who already have their measurements. See [Manual Quote](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-quote).

**Slope multiplier.** A factor applied to a plan measurement to account for ground angle — driveway gradients, batters, sloped retaining wall faces. Rafter-style only.

**Plan measurement.** A measurement taken from a plan or drawing, before any multiplier is applied. See [Plan vs Actual](/docs/concepts/plan-vs-actual).

**Quote builder.** The flow that builds a quote: Areas, Components, Extras, Review. See [Quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder).

**Takeoff.** The act of measuring a job — either digital (from a plan) or manual (on site or from your own notes).

## Billing terms

**Cap.** A numeric limit on something your plan allows - quotes per month, components total, storage bytes, etc. Hitting a cap blocks new additions but never deletes existing data. See [Tier limits](/docs/account/tier-limits).

**Dunning.** The process Stripe (and we) follow when a renewal payment fails: automatic card retries, status escalation through `past_due` - `grace` - `suspended`, with chances to recover by updating the card. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Effective plan.** What your account can actually use today. Usually the same as your purchased plan, but collapses to **Starter** during grace periods or after the trial ends.

**Grace period.** A 10-day window after a payment failure where you keep read access but can't create new quotes. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Trial.** A 14-day free taste of every feature, with tiny caps. One per account, ever. See [Free trial](/docs/account/trial).

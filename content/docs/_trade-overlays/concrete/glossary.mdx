# Glossary

Short definitions of the QuoteCore+ terms and concrete measurement types you will see in the app.

## Concrete measurement types

**Volume.** The primary concrete measure, in m³. Used for ready-mix pours. The app multiplies slab area × slab thickness to get volume automatically when both are set on the component.

**Area.** A flat slab surface in m² - driveways, paths, garages, patios. Drives mesh, finishing, sealer, and per-m² labor.

**Irregular Area.** Non-rectangular slabs - L-shapes, multi-bay slabs, paths with curves.

**Lineal.** A single continuous run measured in metres - kerb, edge strip, expansion joint, sawn cut, perimeter formwork.

**Multiple Kerb/Edge Runs (multi_lineal).** Several kerb or edge runs added together as one component line.

**Curved Kerb/Edge (curved_line).** A curved run measured along its curve - driveway radius, garden bed edge.

**Count.** Individual items - dowels, joint inserts, anchors, drainage fittings.

**Fixed.** A flat amount that doesn't scale with measurement - pump hire, small-load fee, excavation, allowances.

**Hours / Days.** Labor billed by time - pour day, finishing day, supervision.

## QuoteCore+ terms

**Actual measurement.** The real-world measurement after waste and (if relevant) slope have been applied to the plan measurement.

**Component.** A reusable concrete item with its own price, pack size, labor rate, and waste settings.

**Customer quote.** The polished, customer-facing version of the quote. For concrete work the default label is **Concrete Works**.

**Digital takeoff.** Tracing measurements directly on an uploaded site plan or slab drawing.

**Extra.** A non-core component bucket for pump hire, allowances, hidden items, or anything outside the main scope.

**Manual quote.** A quote built by typing measurements straight into the quote builder, without a digital takeoff.

**Pitch multiplier.** A factor that converts plan-view slab area into actual sloped surface area where a significant fall to drainage applies.

**Plan measurement.** The raw measurement taken from the plan, before waste or slope is applied.

**Quote builder.** The screen where you assemble areas, components, prices, and notes into the final quote.

**Takeoff.** The list of measurements that drives the quote - whether digital or manual.

## Billing terms

**Cap.** A numeric limit on something your plan allows - quotes per month, components total, storage bytes, etc. Hitting a cap blocks new additions but never deletes existing data. See [Tier limits](/docs/account/tier-limits).

**Dunning.** The process Stripe (and we) follow when a renewal payment fails: automatic card retries, status escalation through `past_due` - `grace` - `suspended`, with chances to recover by updating the card. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Effective plan.** What your account can actually use today. Usually the same as your purchased plan, but collapses to **Starter** during grace periods or after the trial ends.

**Grace period.** A 10-day window after a payment failure where you keep read access but can't create new quotes. See [Billing](/docs/account/billing#payment-failures-and-dunning).

**Trial.** A 14-day free taste of every feature, with tiny caps. One per account, ever. See [Free trial](/docs/account/trial).

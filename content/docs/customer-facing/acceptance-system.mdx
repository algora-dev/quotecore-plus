# Acceptance system

When a customer clicks the link to your quote, three buttons sit under the document: **Accept**, **Request Changes**, **Decline**. This page covers what happens with each one.

## How it works

Every quote you send has a unique URL. The URL has an expiry date, set when you sent the quote (default 30 days).

When the customer hits the link:

- If the URL is valid, they see the customer quote and the three buttons.
- If the URL is expired, they see an "expired" message with a button to request a fresh quote.
- If you withdrew the quote, they see a "no longer valid" message, also with a request-fresh-quote button.
- If the customer already accepted or declined, they see a confirmation of what they did and when, with a button to request a fresh quote if circumstances changed.

## What each button does

### Accept Quote

The customer clicks Accept. You get:

- An in-app alert.
- An email (if email notifications are on).
- The quote status updates to **Accepted** on the [quotes page](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).

The customer gets a confirmation page. The URL still works for them but only shows the "already accepted" state - they can't accept twice.

### Decline Quote

The customer clicks Decline. You get the alert and the email. The quote status updates to **Declined**.

### Request Changes

The customer clicks Request Changes. They fill in a short note - their name, email, and what they want changed. You get the alert and email with the full message.

This doesn't change the quote status to anything terminal. You read the request, edit the quote, and send a new URL. See [Requote requests](/docs/customer-facing/requote-requests).

## Why we use a link instead of a PDF

A link gives you:

- Status tracking - you know if the customer opened it.
- One-click accept/decline.
- The ability to update the quote and re-send the same way.
- A consistent customer experience whether you sent via email, SMS, or WhatsApp.

You can still attach a PDF if a customer specifically wants one (download it from the customer quote editor).

## Tips

- The expiry date is your friend. If a customer ghosts on a quote for a month, the link expiring gives both of you a clean reset.
- Keep customer email addresses on quotes - it makes the email-from-QuoteCore+ flow much faster.
- Watch for "Request Changes" responses - they're often a chance to win a job that would otherwise have gone to "no response".

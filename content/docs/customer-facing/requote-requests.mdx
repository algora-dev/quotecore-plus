# Requote requests

A requote request is when a customer clicks **Request Changes** on a quote URL. They fill in a short note. You get an alert with the details. You decide what to do next.

## What you receive

When a customer requests changes, you get:

- An in-app alert with their name, the quote number, and their note.
- An email (if email notifications are on) with the same content.
- A new alert in the bell icon, top right of the app.

## What's in the request

The customer fills in:

- Their name (pre-filled if it was on the quote).
- Their email (pre-filled if it was on the quote).
- A short message - what they want changed.

That's it. We keep it short on purpose - longer back-and-forth happens by phone or email.

## What to do next

A typical workflow:

1. Read the request.
2. Reply directly if you want to clarify before changing anything.
3. Open the quote, edit it.
4. Save the customer quote with the changes.
5. From the summary, click **Send Quote** to generate a new URL.
6. Send the new link.

The old link is invalidated automatically when you generate a new one. The customer can't accidentally accept the old version.

## When customers use it vs declining

Most customers who use Request Changes are interested but want a tweak - swap a material, add a line item, change the scope. Treat it as a buying signal, not a rejection.

A request to change is often just a price negotiation in disguise. Decide what you can move on before you respond.

## Tips

- Reply within a working day if you can. Customers appreciate quick turnaround.
- If the change is small, do it and re-send. Don't make them justify the change.
- Keep the customer's note - it's useful context if you don't get the job and want to know why.

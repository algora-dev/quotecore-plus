# Withdrawing a quote

Withdrawing a quote pulls it back from the customer. The quote URL still works, but anyone who clicks it sees a "no longer valid" page with the option to request a fresh quote.

The quote and its data stay in your records. You're just retracting the live link.

## When to use it

- The price was wrong and you don't want the customer accepting it.
- The job changed scope and you need to re-quote.
- You found out you can't do the work.
- The customer never responded and you've moved on.
- You won the job verbally but never got an "accept" click - withdrawing keeps the record clean.

## Steps

1. Open the [quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).
2. Click **Withdraw Quote**.
3. Confirm.

The link's now in withdrawn state. You can re-send a new URL any time and that re-activates the quote with a fresh link.

## What the customer sees

They click the original link and see a "no longer valid" message. There's a button on that page where they can request a fresh quote, with their name, email, and a note. You get an alert if they use it.

This means a withdrawn quote becomes a lead instead of a dead end. If the customer's still interested, you find out.

## Withdrawing vs deleting

- **Withdraw** - pulls back the live link, keeps everything in your records, can be re-activated.
- **Delete** - removes the quote permanently along with attached files. See the bulk delete option on the quotes page.

If you're not sure which to use, withdraw. You can always delete later. Deleting is permanent.

## Tips

- Withdrawing is a good first move on a quote you sent by mistake. You can fix it and re-send a new link.
- Keep withdrawn quotes around for a while - they're useful for follow-up calls.
- If you accidentally withdrew a quote, generate a new URL from the summary and you're back in business.

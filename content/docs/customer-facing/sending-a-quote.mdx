# Sending a quote

Once you've built a customer quote, you can send it to the customer as a unique link. Three ways to do it. All of them give the customer the same Accept / Decline / Request changes buttons.

## Before you start

You need to have built and saved a customer quote on this quote first. See [Customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor).

## Steps

1. From the [quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary), click **Send Quote**.
2. Set how long the link is valid (default is 30 days). After that, customers see an "expired" page when they click.
3. Pick how to send:
   - **Generate URL only** - copy the link, send it however you want (text, your own email, WhatsApp).
   - **Send via your email client** - copy the templated email to clipboard, paste into Gmail/Outlook, send from your address.
   - **Send via QuoteCore+** - we send it from our servers, addressed from your company name with your account email as the reply-to.
4. Confirm.

The customer gets a link. Clicking it opens the customer quote with three buttons at the bottom:

- **Accept Quote**
- **Request Changes**
- **Decline Quote**

You get an in-app alert and (if enabled) an email when they click any of them.

## Send via QuoteCore+

If you pick this option:

- Pick an [email template](/docs/templates/email-templates) (default if you have one).
- The customer email is filled in if you've got it on the quote.
- Hit Send.

The customer gets an email from `noreply` addressed as your company name, with your account email as the reply-to so they can reach you directly. The link is in the body.

We track delivery and clicks. You'll see the status update on the [quotes page](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).

## Send via your own email

Pick this if you want the email to come from *your* address (your customer sees you, not us). We hand you the templated email body as a copy-to-clipboard. You paste it into Gmail or Outlook and send.

The link is the same either way. Acceptance still works the same.

## Generate URL only

For when you want to send via something else - SMS, WhatsApp, your own customer portal. Copy the URL, paste it wherever.

## Re-sending and updating

You can generate a new URL any time. The new URL replaces the old one - if anyone clicks the old link they'll get the "no longer valid" page.

Useful when:

- You needed to fix the quote and want the customer to see the new version.
- The customer lost the email.
- The original link expired.

## Tips

- Default expiry of 30 days is a good balance. Long enough that customers don't feel rushed, short enough that you don't have stale quotes hanging around.
- If you withdraw a quote, the URL still works but tells the customer the quote is no longer valid. See [Withdrawing a quote](/docs/customer-facing/withdrawing-a-quote).
- Always preview the customer quote before sending. The "Open in new tab" link in the editor lets you see exactly what the customer will see.

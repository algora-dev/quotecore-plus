# Quote files

Every quote can have files attached. Roof plans, aerial photos, site photos, building consents, supplier specs - anything you want kept with the quote. Files stay with the quote until you delete them.

## When to attach files

- A roof plan you used for digital takeoff (the plan saves automatically).
- Site photos taken during measure-up.
- Building consents or council documents.
- Supplier spec sheets relevant to the materials.
- Anything you want to refer back to in six months.

## How to attach files

Two places:

### From the quote summary

1. Open the [quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).
2. Scroll to the Files panel at the bottom.
3. Click **Add file**.
4. Pick a file from your computer.
5. Optional: type a short description.
6. Upload.

### From the takeoff/builder

When you're in the [Areas phase](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder), under the quote name there's a dropdown for uploading roof plans or supporting docs.

## What types are accepted

- Images (PNG, JPG).
- PDFs.
- Most common doc formats.

If a file gets rejected, it's usually a size limit - keep individual files under 25 MB.

## How long files stay

Files stay attached as long as:

- The quote exists (deleting the quote deletes its files).
- You haven't manually deleted the file.
- Your subscription's active.
- You're not over your storage limit.

If your subscription lapses for an extended period, files may eventually be deleted. See [Payment issues and storage](/docs/files-and-storage/payment-issues).

## Storage limits

How much you can store depends on your subscription tier. See [Storage tiers](/docs/files-and-storage/storage-tiers).

If you're getting close to the limit, you can:

- Delete files from old or declined quotes.
- Delete declined quotes entirely.
- Upgrade your tier.

## Tips

- Add a short description when you attach a file - helps if you come back to it months later.
- Big files (4K aerial photos) eat your storage fast. Resize before upload if you don't need full resolution.
- Roof plans you used for digital takeoff are saved automatically - you don't need to re-upload them.

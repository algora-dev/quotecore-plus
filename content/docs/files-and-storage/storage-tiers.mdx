# Storage tiers

How much you can store - quotes, files, plans - depends on your subscription. The exact limits and pricing are being finalised before public launch.

<Callout type="note">
This section is being finalised. Pricing and tier details will be published before public launch.
</Callout>

## What counts toward storage

- Files attached to quotes (plans, photos, PDFs).
- Roof plan images saved during digital takeoff.
- Supporting docs uploaded to a quote.
- Logos and assets uploaded to your company settings (small, doesn't add up to much).

What doesn't count:

- Quote text and line items themselves.
- Templates.
- Components.

The numbers are tiny. The files are what take space.

## How to free up storage

When you're getting near your limit:

1. **Delete files from old or declined quotes.** From the quote summary, scroll to the Files panel and delete the ones you don't need.
2. **Delete declined quotes entirely.** From the quotes page, check the box next to a quote and click Delete. This deletes the quote and all its attached files.
3. **Resize images before upload.** A 25 MB photo is rarely needed for documentation - 2 MB is usually plenty.
4. **Download and archive locally.** Quotes you've completed and don't need to reference often can be downloaded as a zip and stored on your own drive. See [Quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).

## How to upgrade

Go to **Account > Billing** and pick a higher tier. Storage limits apply immediately.

See [Billing and subscriptions](/docs/account-and-billing/billing-and-subscriptions).

## Tips

- Keep an eye on your storage usage - the Account page shows where you're at.
- A quick monthly cleanup of declined quotes can save a lot of space.
- If you regularly hit your limit, upgrading is usually cheaper than the time you spend managing storage.

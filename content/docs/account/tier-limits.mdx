# Tier limits

Every plan has caps. This page explains what each one means in practice.

## The five caps

| Cap | What it counts | Reset cycle |
|---|---|---|
| **Quotes / month** | Every quote you create (draft or sent) | 1st of each month, UTC |
| **Components (lifetime)** | Active components in your library | Never — manual delete only |
| **Flashings (lifetime)** | Flashing designs in your library | Never — manual delete only |
| **Material orders / month** | Outgoing orders sent to suppliers | 1st of each month, UTC |
| **Storage** | Total bytes uploaded across the company | Never — manual delete only |

## Per-tier breakdown

| Cap | Trial | Starter | Growth | Pro | Pro Plus |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Quotes / month | 10 | 25 | 50 | 100 | 200 |
| Components | 10 | 10 | 15 | 25 | 50 |
| Flashings | 5 | 0 (locked) | 0 (locked) | 10 | 20 |
| Material orders / month | 5 | 0 (locked) | 0 (locked) | 10 | 20 |
| Storage | 100 MB | 200 MB | 2 GB | 3 GB | 5 GB |
| Included seats | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 3 |

"Locked" means the feature itself is not available on that tier — not zero, but no access at all (the navigation hides the page).

## Quotes per month

The most important cap. Both **draft quotes** and **finalised quotes** count toward this limit. A new quote consumes one slot when it's created.

<Callout type="warning">
Sending or finalising a draft does **not** free up its slot. The counter is "quotes created", not "quotes pending". This is intentional — it prevents people from cycling drafts to dodge the cap.
</Callout>

When you hit the monthly cap:

- The **+ New Quote** button on the quotes list page greys out.
- Clicking it opens an upgrade modal: *"Monthly quote limit reached (X/Y). To create more quotes this month you need to upgrade your account tier, or wait until your quote limit resets."*
- The cap resets at midnight UTC on the 1st of next month.

## Components (lifetime)

Components are the building blocks of every quote (ridge cap, valley flashing, fascia, etc.). The lifetime cap counts every **active** component in your library.

- Soft-deleted components (set inactive) **do not count**. Hard-deleting is the only way to make room if you've toggled instead of deleted.
- New accounts are seeded with 8 starter components, so you start at 8 of your limit.
- When at the cap, the **+ Add Component** button on the components page opens an upgrade modal.

## Flashings (lifetime)

Same model as components, but for the flashings library. Flashings are images + drawings of cut metal pieces that go onto material orders.

- Every drawing or uploaded image counts as 1.
- Editing an existing flashing does not consume a new slot.
- When at the cap, both **Create** and **Upload** buttons on the flashings page open an upgrade modal.

On **Starter** and **Growth**, flashings is a **locked feature** — the whole page shows an upgrade splash and the "Flashings" entry button on the components page opens an upgrade modal instead of navigating.

## Material orders per month

Outgoing purchase orders sent to suppliers. Resets monthly like quotes.

On **Starter** and **Growth**, material orders is a **locked feature** — the nav entry opens an upgrade modal and doesn't route through.

## Storage

Total bytes uploaded to your company storage. Counts:

- Roof plan PDFs and images uploaded for digital takeoff
- Photos attached to quotes
- Company logo
- Flashing reference images

Does **not** count:

- Generated PDFs (created on the fly when you click download)
- Customer-facing accept page renders

When you're near the limit:

- A storage usage bar on the billing page shows your usage.
- At 90% it turns amber.
- At 100% the next upload fails with a "Storage quota exceeded" error.

To free up space, delete old quote attachments or roof plans you no longer need.

## How the upgrade modal works

Every cap opens the same modal pattern:

- Title naming the specific cap you hit
- Description explaining what's blocked
- **Not now** button to dismiss
- **View plans** button that takes you to **Account > Billing** with the recommended plan highlighted

## What about grandfathered data?

If you signed up on a higher tier then downgraded, you may end up with **more existing data than your new tier allows**. That's fine:

- We never delete data when you downgrade.
- Caps only block *new* additions.
- Example: a customer with 30 components who downgrades from Pro (25-component cap) to Growth (15-component cap) keeps all 30 components. They just can't add a new one until they delete enough to get back under 15.

## Where to see your current usage

- **Billing page** — storage bar.
- **Quotes page** — quotes-this-month counter (top right of the list).
- **Components page** — `X/Y` counter inside the + Add Component button.
- **Flashings page** — `X/Y used` next to the Create/Upload buttons.

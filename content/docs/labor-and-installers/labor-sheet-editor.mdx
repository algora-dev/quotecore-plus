# Labor sheet editor

The labor sheet is the install version of a quote. Same line items as the customer quote, but only the labor amounts (and quantities the fixers actually need to know about). It's what your installers work from on site.

## When to use it

- After a quote is accepted and you're ready to schedule the install.
- Before install, to share scope with your fixer or crew.
- For pricing your install team's pay against actual install hours.

## Opening it

From the [quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary), click **Labor Sheet**.

## What you see

The editor looks similar to the [customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor) but with a few differences:

- Material prices are hidden by default. Labor prices are shown.
- The header and footer come from your [labor sheet template](/docs/templates/labor-sheet-templates).
- You can show or hide individual lines if a fixer doesn't need to see something.
- You can add custom notes - install instructions, site access, special requirements.

## Editing

- **Show/hide** lines you do or don't want on the install document.
- **Edit descriptions** if you need to add detail for the fixer (e.g. "use stainless fixings" on a coastal job).
- **Pick a labor sheet template** from the dropdown to swap header/footer layouts.
- **Add custom lines** for install-specific notes that aren't in the master quote.

## Saving and sharing

- **Save** stores the labor sheet on the quote.
- **Download** gives you a PDF.
- **Print** for paper-on-site use.

For the digital handoff process, see [Sharing with fixers](/docs/labor-and-installers/sharing-with-fixers).

## Tips

- Add scope notes in the footer template so they appear on every labor sheet.
- If your fixers prefer paper, download and print on the day.
- Keep the labor sheet up to date if scope changes during the job.

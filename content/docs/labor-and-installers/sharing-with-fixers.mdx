# Sharing with fixers

A few ways to get the labor sheet to your install crew. Pick whichever fits how your team works.

## Options

### Download a PDF and send it

The simplest. From the [labor sheet editor](/docs/labor-and-installers/labor-sheet-editor), click **Download**. You get a PDF. Send via email, message, WhatsApp, whatever your team uses.

Pros: works for any crew, even if they don't use the app.
Cons: if you update the quote, you need to re-send.

### Print and hand over

Old-school but reliable. Print and give it to the fixer at the start of the job.

Pros: paper survives wet hands and dropped phones.
Cons: when scope changes you're tracking down the paper version.

### Open the labor sheet in the app on a phone

If your crew has access to the workspace, they can open the labor sheet directly in the app. (This requires team accounts, which are [coming soon](/docs/account-and-billing/team-members).)

Until team accounts ship, share via PDF or print.

## What to include in the labor sheet

A useful labor sheet for fixers usually has:

- Site address.
- Customer name and contact (so the fixer can reach the customer if needed).
- Your contact details.
- Each component with quantity and any install notes.
- Site-specific notes - access, hours, hazards.

Most of this comes from your [labor sheet template](/docs/templates/labor-sheet-templates). Set it up once and every labor sheet is consistent.

## Tips

- Include site access and parking notes in the footer of every labor sheet template - small detail, saves real time on site.
- If you've got photos of the job (from digital takeoff or attached files), include the relevant ones with the labor sheet.

# FAQ

Common questions, short answers, links if you want more.

## What's the difference between a Component and an Extra?

Components are the materials and labor you'd order from a supplier - tiles, longrun, ridges, fixings. Extras are everything else - skip hire, accommodation, scaffold, custom one-offs. Both behave the same for pricing; the split is mostly about how the quote reads. See [Components vs Extras](/docs/core-concepts/components-vs-extras).

## Can I edit a sent quote?

Yes. Either:

- Edit the customer quote directly (small text changes) in the [customer quote editor](/docs/customer-facing/customer-quote-editor) and save.
- Edit the underlying quote (numbers and components) by going back into the [quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder) from the summary.

After either, generate a fresh URL from the summary so the customer sees the new version. The old URL becomes invalid.

## How do I handle a re-roof where I have actual measurements?

Use [manual takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-takeoff). Add your components, type in the measurements, and set each component to **Actual** so the pitch maths get skipped. Waste still applies. See [Plan vs Actual](/docs/core-concepts/plan-vs-actual).

## Can the customer see my labor cost?

No - not unless you specifically choose to show it. The [labor sheet](/docs/labor-and-installers/labor-sheet-editor) is separate from the customer quote. Labor amounts on the customer quote are bundled into the line price by default.

## What if I get the pitch wrong?

Two ways to fix it:

- If you set the wrong pitch on an area, edit the area in the [quote builder](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-builder) and the maths re-run.
- If a component should have had pitch enabled and didn't (or vice versa), edit the component, then re-add it to the quote builder. The quote re-calculates.

If you've already sent the quote, generate a new URL after fixing.

## How do I clone a quote?

From the [quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary), click **Clone Quote**. Enter a new customer name. Edit anything that needs changing.

Cloning is faster than starting fresh when:

- You're quoting a similar job.
- You messed up a quote and want a clean restart.
- You're quoting the same job for a different customer.

## What happens if my customer doesn't respond?

Three options:

- Wait. The quote URL is valid for whatever expiry you set (default 30 days).
- Follow up manually with a phone call or email.
- Once [follow-up emails](/docs/customer-facing/follow-up-emails) ship, you'll be able to set automatic chase emails.

If the link expires before they respond, the customer can request a fresh quote from the expired-link page.

## Does the system work on iPad / on site?

Yes. The app works on tablets and phones for most flows - reading quotes, status updates, generating URLs. The digital takeoff drawing tool is best on a tablet or laptop with a stylus or trackpad. Drawing complex roofs on a phone is technically possible but slow.

## What measurement systems are supported?

Three:

- **Metric** - meters, m2, mm.
- **Imperial feet** - feet, ft2, inches.
- **Imperial roofing squares** - linear feet, roofing squares (Rs), with conversions handled automatically.

Pick a default in [Company settings](/docs/account-and-billing/company-settings). Each new quote locks to its system at creation. Existing quotes don't change if you change the default later.

## How do I add a one-off cost like skip hire?

Add it as an **Extra**. Quickest way: add it as a line item directly in the quote builder Extras phase. If it's something you'll use on multiple quotes, save it as a component with type "Extra" so it's in your library.

## Can two people in my company work on the same quote?

Not yet - team accounts are [coming soon](/docs/account-and-billing/team-members). Right now there's one login per company. You can share a login if you really need to, but each person should ideally have their own when team accounts ship.

## How do I delete a quote?

From the [quotes page](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary), check the box next to the quote(s), then click **Delete**. Once deleted, the quote and all attached files are gone permanently.

If you might want it later, [withdraw](/docs/customer-facing/withdrawing-a-quote) instead.

## Why is my customer quote total different from my summary?

Two possibilities:

- The customer quote was edited after the master was last saved (common - you tweaked descriptions or hid lines).
- The summary shows everything; the customer quote may have hidden lines that still count toward the total.

Open the customer quote editor and the summary side-by-side. Compare line by line.

## How do I back up everything?

From the [quotes page](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary), check the box next to one or many quotes, click **Download**. You get a zip containing the quotes, customer quotes, labor sheets, and all attached files. Drop it on your computer, a USB stick, or your cloud drive.

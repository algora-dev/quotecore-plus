# Quote builder

The quote builder is where a quote actually gets put together. It runs in four phases. You can move forward and backward through them at any time before you save.

## The four phases

### 1. Areas

Add the named sections of the roof. Most jobs have at least one ("Main roof"). Bigger jobs might have a Main, a Garage, a Lean-to, a Dormer.

For each area:

- **Name** - whatever makes sense.
- **Pitch** - the angle of the slope. Used by any component on this area that has rafter pitch enabled. See [Waste, pitch, and calculations](/docs/core-concepts/waste-pitch-and-calculations).
- **Plan area** - the flat number off the plan, in m2 or whatever your default is.

Areas exist so you can mix pitches on a single quote. A garage at 15 degrees alongside a main roof at 30 degrees - two areas, two pitches.

### 2. Components

Add the components that go on each area. For each one:

- Pick which area(s) it applies to.
- Enter the measurement (or it's pre-filled from digital takeoff).
- Choose **Plan** or **Actual**. See [Plan vs Actual](/docs/core-concepts/plan-vs-actual).
- Override the rate, waste, or pitch if you need to (they default to your component's saved settings).

You can have a component apply to multiple areas - useful for a single supplier order even if the materials cover different sections.

### 3. Extras

Add any extras for the quote. Skip hire, accommodation, callouts, custom one-offs. See [Components vs Extras](/docs/core-concepts/components-vs-extras).

Extras don't usually use pitch or waste maths, but they can if you set them up that way.

### 4. Review

The last stop before save. Here you'll see:

- Subtotal of materials.
- Subtotal of labor.
- Margin fields (overrides your company defaults).
- Tax line(s).
- Final total.

Tweak the margins per quote here. Click Save to lock the quote and open the [quote summary](/docs/building-a-quote/quote-summary).

## Going back to edit

You can re-enter the quote builder from the summary page (**Edit Quote**). Doing so puts you back at the Areas phase. Walk through to whatever you want to change, then save again.

<Callout type="note">
Re-entering the builder doesn't break anything - your customer quote and labor sheet just get re-flagged for review. If you'd already sent the customer a quote, send a fresh one or [withdraw](/docs/customer-facing/withdrawing-a-quote) the old link.
</Callout>

## Tips

- Set Plan/Actual deliberately on every component - it's the most common source of wrong totals.
- Use multiple areas if pitches differ. One area with mixed pitches forces an awkward average.
- Margins on the Review phase override company defaults. Set company defaults to your typical numbers and only override when something's special.

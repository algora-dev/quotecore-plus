# Digital takeoff

Digital takeoff lets you measure off a digital plan or aerial image without printing it out. Calibrate once, then draw your areas and lengths with your mouse.

## When to use it

- You've got a digital roof plan, aerial image, or PDF you can upload.
- You want to measure on screen instead of paper.
- You want a saved drawing of what you measured to refer back to.

You need a known reference measurement on the plan to calibrate against. A scale bar, a known wall length, anything you trust.

## Steps

1. **Upload the plan.** From New Quote, pick **Digital takeoff**, set customer and job reference, click Create.
2. **Calibrate.** The first thing you do is pick two points with a known distance between them on the plan. Type the real-world distance. The app calibrates so every line you draw afterwards is to scale.
3. **Add an area.** Click "Add area", give it a name ("Main roof", "Garage"), set the pitch.
4. **Draw the area outline.** Click around the perimeter of the section you're measuring. Close the shape. The app calculates the area for you.
5. **Add components to the area.** For each component, you'll either:
   - Draw lines (for lineal items - ridges, valleys, gutters).
   - Place points (for quantity items - downpipes, skylights).
   - Use the area number directly (for area items - the main roofing material covers the whole area you drew).
6. **Repeat for other areas.** Add a second roof section, draw it, add its components.
7. **Continue.** When everything's drawn, click through to the quote builder. The components are pre-filled with measurements.

## Calibration

Calibration matters more than anything else. If your reference distance is wrong, every measurement on the quote is wrong.

- Use a scale bar if the plan has one.
- If not, pick a wall or distance you know is correct.
- Long reference distances are more accurate than short ones - small errors get amplified less.

<Callout type="warning">
Don't trust scale bars on photos or scanned plans without checking. Sometimes scans get resized. Pick a real-world distance you measured yourself if you can.
</Callout>

## What gets saved

- Your original plan image.
- A digital takeoff overlay - all your lines, areas, and points marked.
- Every measurement, ready for the quote builder.

You can print the takeoff to use as a working sheet on site, or refer back to it months later if you need to remember what you measured.

## Tips

- Zoom in for accuracy on small details. Zoom doesn't affect calibration.
- Re-calibrate if you replace the plan image.
- Lineal items show their length as you draw - watch the running total.
- If a component doesn't have pitch enabled but should, fix the component setting and start that line again.

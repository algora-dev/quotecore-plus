# Manual quote

The manual quote builder is for trades that don't measure roofs - or for quotes where you've got the numbers and just want to type them in. No takeoff, no pitch, no waste maths unless you want it.

## When to use it

- You're not roofing.
- You've got a quote scribbled on paper from a site visit.
- You just want a clean, professional document and don't need the takeoff workflow.
- You're quoting a one-off job that doesn't fit your usual component flow.

If you're roofing, you probably want [Manual takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/manual-takeoff) or [Digital takeoff](/docs/building-a-quote/digital-takeoff) instead.

## Steps

1. Go to **Quotes > New Quote**.
2. Enter customer name and (optional) job reference.
3. Pick **Manual quote**.
4. Click **Create**.
5. The manual quote builder opens. Add line items.
6. Each line is free-form: a description, a quantity, a unit, and a price. You can also drop in saved components from your library.
7. Save when done.
8. Build the customer quote and labor sheet from the summary as normal.

## Line items

- **Description** - what the line is for.
- **Quantity and unit** - 5 hours, 100 m, 1 each, whatever fits.
- **Price** - per unit.
- **Total** - calculated.
- **Show price** toggle - hide the price on the customer quote without removing the line.

You can pull in components you've already built. They'll come with their saved rates.

## Tips

- If you find yourself writing the same lines over and over, save them as components - even for a non-roofing trade. The library still helps.
- Manual quotes can have Extras the same as roofing quotes. Use them for delivery, callouts, anything one-off.

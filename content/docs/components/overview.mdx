# Components overview

Components are your re-usable price book. Anything you put on a quote - tiles, longrun, ridges, valleys, fixings, underlay, scaffold - lives here once. After that, building a quote is picking from your library and entering measurements.

## Why components matter

The first time you set them up, it takes an hour. Every quote after that is faster, more consistent, and harder to get wrong. You change a price once and every new quote uses the new price.

You can have four components or four hundred. The library scales with the work.

## What's in a component

Each component holds:

- A name (what shows on the quote).
- Whether it's a [Main component or an Extra](/docs/core-concepts/components-vs-extras).
- A measurement type - area, lineal, quantity, or fixed.
- A material price per unit.
- A labor price per unit.
- A waste setting.
- An optional pitch type ([rafter, hip, or valley](/docs/core-concepts/waste-pitch-and-calculations)).
- Whether it can be added to material orders.
- An attached flashing drawing or image (optional).

For the full breakdown of every field, see [Component fields reference](/docs/components/component-fields-reference).

## Examples

- **Roofing iron.** Area-based, material rate per m2, labor rate per m2, 5% waste, rafter pitch.
- **Ridge.** Lineal, material rate per linear meter, labor rate per linear meter, 100mm fixed waste per length, no pitch.
- **Valley.** Lineal, valley pitch enabled.
- **Skylight.** Quantity, fixed price per unit, no waste, no pitch.
- **Site cleanup.** Extra, fixed cost.

## How components show up on a quote

When you build a quote (manual takeoff or digital takeoff), you pick components from your library. The app pre-fills the rates, waste, and pitch you saved. You enter the measurement (or it gets pre-filled from digital takeoff). The quote builder takes care of the maths.

You can override anything on a per-quote basis without changing the saved component. So you can have a default 5% waste in your library and dial it up to 7% for one specific quote.

## When you'd add a new component

- You bought new product and want to quote it.
- You're quoting a job with a specific material you don't usually use.
- You want a custom labor item separate from your normal rate.
- A supplier changed pricing and you need a new variant.

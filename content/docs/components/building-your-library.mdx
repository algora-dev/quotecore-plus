# Building your component library

You'll get the most out of QuoteCore+ when your components reflect how you actually work. There's no right number - some users have 10 components, some have 200. What matters is consistency.

## Start small, expand over time

Don't try to build the whole library on day one. Build the ten or twenty things you put on every quote. Quote a few jobs. Add new components when you need them.

If you find yourself manually typing the same line into multiple quotes, that's a sign it should be a component.

## How to name components

Specific names beat clever names. Compare:

- "Longrun" vs "Longrun corrugate 0.55 colorsteel ironsand"
- "Ridge" vs "Ridge cap - 240 standard pre-painted"
- "Underlay" vs "Underlay - synthetic permeable 1.5x50m"

The longer name takes one more second to read. It saves you fifteen minutes when you can't remember which "longrun" was the right one for a specific quote three months later.

## How to split variants

If you sell something in two finishes, two thicknesses, or two profiles, make two components. Don't try to handle it with notes or by editing the price on each quote.

- Tiles: terracotta clay, concrete, slate - three components.
- Longrun: corrugate 0.4, corrugate 0.55, trapezoidal 0.55 - three components.
- Underlay: synthetic, foil, breathable - three components.

You can clone components if they share most of their fields - clone, then edit the price and name.

## How to handle labor

You have a few options:

- **Bake labor into each material component.** A "Roofing tiles" component has a material rate of $X/m2 and a labor rate of $Y/m2. Most users do this.
- **Separate labor components.** A "Tile install labor" component you add separately on each quote. Useful if labor pricing varies by site rather than by material.
- **Mix.** Most components have labor; specific labor lines (callouts, after-hours premiums) live as separate Extras.

Pick one approach and stick with it. Switching mid-library makes quotes inconsistent.

## What to put as Extras

- Skip hire and site cleanup.
- Scaffold (unless you measure it as area or lineal).
- Accommodation, travel, callouts.
- Discounts.
- Optional add-ons you want shown to the customer with a separate price.

See [Components vs Extras](/docs/core-concepts/components-vs-extras) for more detail.

## Reviewing your library

Once a year, scan your component list:

- Delete components you haven't used in 12 months.
- Update prices that have shifted.
- Rename anything that's gotten ambiguous.
- Promote frequently-edited inline lines into proper components.

A tight library beats a sprawling one.
